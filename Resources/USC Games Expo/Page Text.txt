Overview

The USC Games Expo is an annual showcase event hosted by the USC Games program. This expo brings together students, educators, industry professionals, and the general public to celebrate the amazing games our student and alumni have produced throughout the previous year. This year we are celebrating the 10th anniversary of the USC Games Expo and are poised to have the most attendees and submissions in the expo's history.

My Role
As Project Manager, I am responsible for the overall success of the USC Games Expo by assisting the Expo Director. Together we have successfully built and maintained a thriving expo that has been the culmination of year's worth of student games.

Responsibilities

Scheduling and Coordination - managed, assigned, and assisted a group of 50+ jury members in judging student submissions for this expo
Expo layout design - created layouts of the expo grounds which incorporated over 80 booths from varying parties all within site regulations
Managed Showcases - directly oversaw the successful showcase of our games in a sneak preview to potential new university students, industry personnel, and educators
Director Support - supported the Expo Director with any additional tasks that are required to ensure a successful event

Challenges
An expo of this magnitude is not without some obstacles. The key obstacle that I wanted to highlight is management and delegation of resources.

Delegation of Resources
Although USC is incredibly fortunate to have the resources to put on an event of this size, we must work within a budget as well as work with a finite amount of resources.
